THE ORIGIN

Origin

I have always been drawn to things that are hard to explain.

Growing up, I often felt caught between two worlds. Being born and raised in a Western environment as the only Asian kid around me, I became familiar with what it felt like to be different. I often felt like I was standing slightly outside the frame—present, but never fully seen.

Because of that, I started noticing things other people often overlooked: small shifts in expression, silence, and the emotions hidden underneath words. I learned that even when nothing seems visible on the surface, there is often so much happening underneath.

Expression

When words failed me, art became my voice.

There were many thoughts and emotions I struggled to express, especially during times when I felt isolated or misunderstood. Creating became the one place where I didn’t have to explain myself to be understood.

Through art, I found a way to release emotions that felt too complicated or too heavy to put into words. It became more than just a hobby—it became both an outlet and a reflection of who I was.

Projection

Because of this, I naturally put parts of myself into everything I create.

Every character, visual idea, and story I make carries pieces of my own experiences, emotions, and questions. My work is deeply personal, even when it doesn’t seem obvious at first.

Even if a story is fictional, the emotions behind it are real.

From Idea to World

I am fascinated by the process of giving shape to things that cannot be seen.

A lot of my ideas begin as feelings, fleeting thoughts, or random mental images that are difficult to explain. What I love most about animation and concept creation is turning those fragments into something real—something people can see, feel, and connect with.

I especially enjoy the early stages of creating, when ideas are still rough and full of possibilities. I love experimenting with symbolism, visual metaphors, color, and composition to build meaning beyond what is directly shown.

Vision

I create not just to make something visually appealing, but to make people feel seen.

I want my work to resonate with people who have felt misunderstood, disconnected, or caught between identities. I believe art can create space for emotions that are hard to describe but deeply human.

Through every sketch, frame, and story, I continue exploring the space between what is visible and what is felt.
